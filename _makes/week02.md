<h2>Q and A</h2>

<p><strong>What does “authenticity” mean when identity is co-constructed with AI?
Where do you see yourself in the image — and where do you not?</strong><br>
Authenticity loses some of its meaning when our work is being co-created by artificial intelligence. In creative realms like writing, using AI can take all of the personality and authenticity out of our work. Our words are no longer our own, they are a random string that a program determined was the best fit of a pattern it took from a set. However, when used as a tool, AI doesn’t have to suck all the personal touches out of a project. It can be used to complement a person's work and make things possible in seconds rather than weeks. It can create images that before would’ve remained a thought in someone's head, or crunch numbers in seconds that would’ve taken any human hours by hand. 

I can see my likeness in the image that the AI created, although it tries to smooth out some features. Flaws that add realism in the original photo are lost when the AI touches it up, because that is more similar to the photos that the model was trained on. Through a series of infinite small changes, something doesn’t look right. I can tell that the image is of me, but it doesn’t feel quite right. 

</p>
