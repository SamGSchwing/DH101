<div class="card-section">
<h2>Reflection</h2>
<p>


When creating these images, I was looking for a combination of aesthetic and technical execution

One base prompt: Create an image of a red sunset in the reflection of a skyscraper
10-20 systematic variations (change one element at a time): I went through a lot of variation, including but not limited to; changed one word of the prompt at a time, adding and leaving words out, and using different websites to generate the images for me (DALLE3, Midjourney, ace.ai, ChatGPT) 

My thought process with my original prompt was to make something that could be really beautiful if executed well, but pretty technically and conceptually difficult.

 My best result (above) does a really good job with the sky scraper, water textures, and overall image quality, but the reflection in the skyscraper doesn’t exactly match what you would expect to see in real life. 

I also like this one that is done in the style of an oil painting. Even though there is no skyscraper, the reflection aspect is maintained in the water. 

</div>

1. At what point did YOU make a creative decision? List specific moments.
I made creative choices,
When I chose the subject and prompts for the images. 
I also chose the style of the image (realistic, oil painting)
I chose which images were the best out of 15+
2. If you showed someone your curated outputs without explanation, would they know a human selected them?
Probably not. If they had no explanation that it was a curated selection, they would have no reason to believe that they were picked out of a group. 
3. What would "pure" AI creativity look like? What would "pure" human creativity look like? Where does your work fall?
There is no such thing as “pure” AI creativity in my opinion. But AI generation includes high volume, random creation. Human creativity is much slower by comparison, but with real feelings and meanings behind the creation. 
4. Did you ever feel overwhelmed by an infinite generation? When did you know to stop?
I knew that it was time to stop the generation when I felt like I had seen the images that were being generated before.
