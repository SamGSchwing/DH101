<h2>Research Notes</h2>
<p>I used a lot of sources for my research, including wikipedia, wise.com, utopia analytics, and glassdoor.com. My favorite was Glassdoor, which gives good insights on companies as well as information that the companies might not want you to find. The hardest thing to find was probably the exact companies that are responsible for the outsourcing of the labor. It was hard to find because big companies will hire tiny ones to do the things that they want done but they don't want to be held accountable for. Something that surprised me was the degree of harm which the people responsible for this type of work suffer from. I had no idea that serious psychological effects were so common for people in this field of work, some even having conditions like PTSD from the content they must consume. Something else that surprised me was the existence of mass human moderation teams. I thought that a algorithm was most likely responsible and it would just take down a post that was deemed harmful based on the amount of times it had been reported for inappropriate content.</p>
</div>

<div class="card-section">
<h2>Design Statement</h2>
<p>I chose this format because I was familiar with the process of using the website. I have never heard of zine but I have made an infographic before so I decided to go with the option with which I personally felt the most comfortable. I think that my infographic would be best used in online areas where moderation takes place and is done by real people. This is because I think that there is a giant lack of recognition and I think that most people most likely do not know the types of content that real people have to put up with in order to maintain a safe site for the average user. I would want the viewer of my infographic to come away first of all knowing about the existence of these people and also in the future, when they use a site like this they also think about the invisible work that goes into keeping it a safe and appropriate place.</p>
</div>
