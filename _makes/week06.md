Research 

To research for my map, I mostly used google searches. I learned about manufacturing sites like TSMC, Intel, and Samsung electronics. I traced who they source their raw materials from and it was a lot of mines owned by Chinese tycoons and wealthy Europeans and Americans. I also learned about mines like Kolwezi and the Greenbushes Lithium Mine. One of the hardest things to find was the owners of the mines in Africa. Most sources discussed the mines in relation to who they sell to and not who they are owned by. This gap reveals that the supply chains for AI are often discussed separately than the AI specifically. It was also hard to find the exact location, instead getting the town or settlement name nearby the mines. It surprised me how widespread these supply chains are. It spans many continents and very far east and west. 
Artist Statement
This map shows the physical infrastructure behind the creation and upkeep of AI. When people talk about AI, they never talk about the creation process, they talk about ethical concerns or new chatbot updates. These systems are very important to discuss, but often leave out a huge aspect of the truth. A clear pattern emerges when all the locations are mapped on the same map. The raw materials are extracted from the global south in areas like africa. Then they are turned into chips and wafers in places like Taiwan and South Korea, then sold to places like the US and EU. The headquarters and research development happens in the US, almost solely in the area known as silicon valley.
