  <h1>Sustainability</h1>
  <p>Sustainability is often discussed in terms of protecting the environment, but it also involves considering the long-term social, economic, and technological impacts of the systems we rely on every day. In DH101, sustainability connects to questions about how digital technologies are created, maintained, and consumed, as well as the hidden environmental costs behind modern computing and online infrastructure.</p>

  <p>This course has encouraged me to think more critically about the relationship between technology and sustainability. While digital tools can improve communication, accessibility, and efficiency, they also require large amounts of energy, physical resources, and labor to operate. Data centers, electronic waste, and the rapid pace of technological consumption all contribute to environmental challenges that are often overlooked.</p>

  <p>At the same time, sustainability is not only about reducing harm, but also about creating systems that are adaptable, accessible, and responsible over time. Through this website and my coursework, I aim to reflect on how technology can be used more thoughtfully and how digital spaces can encourage awareness, creativity, and long-term thinking.</p>
